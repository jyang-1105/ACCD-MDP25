Complex Motion Rhythm

Exploring timing, rhythm, and organic motion through p5.js

Final Interactive Sketch

Process Documentation

iter 1 — basic circular motion

In the first iteration, I built a simple circular system. A small planet moves around the center using trigonometric functions. This helped me understand basic periodic motion.

iter 2 — adding organic shape

I added a jelly-like form generated with Perlin noise. The movement feels soft and slightly unpredictable, introducing a more natural rhythm.

iter 3 — timed intervals

Here I made the jelly move after specific time intervals. It creates pauses and restarts, which makes the motion feel less mechanical and more alive.

iter 4 — de-synchronization

I started to desynchronize the planet and jelly. Both follow different rhythms, so their motions rarely repeat in the same way. This adds complexity and visual interest.

iter 5 — space background

To give depth, I added a starfield and foggy nebula background. The scene now feels like a small world floating in space.

iter 6 — final rainbow jelly

In the final version, the jelly changes its color randomly across frames, becoming a vibrant rainbow shape that represents dynamic rhythm and energy.
