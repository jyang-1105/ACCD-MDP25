Holographic Advertising Pod

A Speculative Design for Future Retail Spaces

Introduction

This project explores possible futures for holographic display technology. I used a 2D matrix to map out different scenarios and focused on commercial advertising pods, standalone devices that could show up in retail spaces within 10-15 years.

Future Matrix

Chosen Future Scenario

Position: Plausible (10-15 years)

I chose Commercial Advertising Pods from the bottom-left quadrant. This is a near-future where stores use standalone holographic devices to show products and promotions.

Why this scenario?

  • The tech already exists in early versions
  • Companies would invest in it (advertising = money)
  • Easier to build than fully integrated systems
  • Realistic but still innovative

Design Concept

A cylindrical device for retail spaces with:

  • Transparent chamber for holographic projection
  • Scalable size adaptable to most retail environments
  • Blue colors for tech feel
  • Visible technical parts

The empty transparent space shows where holographic content would appear - product images, text, etc. that customers could see from any angle.

Modeling Process

Built in Blender:

  • Basic cylinder shapes for structure
  • Added control panels, ports, base
  • Made the display chamber transparent
  • Applied glass materials
  • Set up lighting and camera

Final Thoughts

This project made me think about how new tech might change our spaces. The holographic ad pod is one possible future, realistic enough to actually happen.

It could make shopping more interesting, but also raises questions about visual clutter and privacy in public spaces.
