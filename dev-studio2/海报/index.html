All Water
Meets Again
Camellia  ·  In response to Hermann Hesse

A browser-based interactive piece in dialogue with Hermann Hesse. The cursor generates a warm amber river; a cold blue river already exists on screen. When the two come close enough, their colors merge. Once enough particles have changed, the text advances — until a transition sentence marks the shift in voice: "All the water in the world will meet again — but Hesse never mentioned the wind." From this point, the particles are gradually dispersed. They do not respawn. The screen slowly empties as the final lines appear.

Hermann Hesse
All the water in the world will meet again —
the Arctic Ocean and the Nile River
will blend in the wet clouds.
Transition
All the water in the world will meet again —
but Hesse never mentioned the wind.
Camellia
The wind doesn't need to.
The wind just moves — through you, past you, and on.
Not as loss. Just as motion.
