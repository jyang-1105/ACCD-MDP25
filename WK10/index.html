About the Game

🌤
World & Setting
A 2D pixel art game made in Unity. The world has blue skies, water, and grassy platforms — pretty classic platformer setting but it fits the sponge theme well.
🏡
The Goal
Get through the level, deal with the enemies along the way, and make it to the wooden cottage at the end to win. It sounds simple but you have to think about whether you're wet or dry the whole time.
🧽
The Sponge
You control a sponge. That might sound random but it actually makes a lot of sense for the mechanics — sponges absorb water, and in this game that absorption changes everything about how you play.
🐞
Enemies
Ladybugs walk back and forth on the ground, and eagles fly around in the air. They need different strategies — a dry sponge can take out a ladybug no problem, but eagles are tougher and you need the extra weight from being wet to bring them down.

How It Works

01
Wetness & Physics State System
How much water the sponge has absorbed changes its actual physics values in Unity. It's the core mechanic I built the whole game around. Being dry is faster and jumpier, being wet is slower but hits harder — and you're constantly switching between the two depending on what's ahead.
🌟 Dry State
Lightweight, fast movement, higher jumps. Great for covering ground quickly and reaching spots that need height or speed.
💧 Wet State
Slower and heavier, but hits hard. Good for taking down tougher enemies that a light sponge can't handle.
02
Water Droplet Collection
Blue water droplets float around the level and you jump to collect them. The counter in the top left shows how many you've grabbed. The more you collect, the heavier you get — so picking up droplets isn't just for points, it actually changes what you can do.
03
Physics-Based Stomp Combat
It's pretty simple — jump on top of enemies to beat them. But whether it actually works depends on how heavy you are. That's where the wet/dry system comes in. Some enemies go down easily, others need you to be fully soaked first.
Ladybug Dry OK
Patrols on the ground. A standard stomp from a dry sponge carries enough impact to knock it out — no extra preparation needed.
Eagle Needs Wet
Flies around in the air. Has pretty high defense, so a dry stomp won't do much. You need to absorb enough water first, then drop on it.

State Management
The main thing to figure out is when to stay dry and when to soak up. Dry is better for moving around and jumping over gaps. Switch to wet right before you need to stomp a tougher enemy. If you're too heavy you'll have trouble making some jumps, so it's a constant back and forth.
