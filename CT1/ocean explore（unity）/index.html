Ocean Explorer

An underwater world built in Unity where you swim around, collect shells, and click on creatures to learn what they are. The goal was to make something that felt calm and open rather than goal-driven.

Sketches

I started with a rough sketch of the scene layout and a diagram mapping out the controls and the two main interactions before building anything.

Environment

The scene is built on a Unity terrain with low-poly coral and rock assets. I added underwater fog and adjusted the lighting to get a blue-green tint. Fish and sea turtles move around on their own using a raycast-based AI so they don't just walk into walls.

Interactions

Collect Shells

Walk up to a shell and click it. It disappears and the score goes up by one. Simple, but satisfying to do repeatedly.

Identify Creatures

Click on any fish or sea turtle and a panel slides in with the creature's name and a short description. Click close to go back to exploring.

Code

The main scripts I wrote for this project — the fish movement AI was the most complex part, the rest handles collection and UI.

OceanAnimalMovement — Smart Turn

This is the part I spent the most time on. It shoots three raycasts (left, forward, right) and decides which direction to turn based on which ones hit something. If the front is blocked it checks left and right and picks the open side. If everything is blocked it just picks a random angle.

CollectManager

Keeps track of the score and updates the text on screen every time something is collected.

CollectObject

When you click a shell, this script finds the CollectManager, tells it to add a point, then destroys the shell object.

ShowManager

Fills in the info panel with the creature's name and description, and moves the camera to look at the creature you clicked.

Fish

Tracks whether the info panel is open or not. First click opens the panel, clicking again plays a little bounce animation using DOTween.
